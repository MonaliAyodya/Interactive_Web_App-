Carrot and Coriander Soup

Description

This Carrot and Coriander Soup is a fragrant and healthy dish, perfect for chilly days. It blends sweet carrots with fresh coriander and a hint of spice for a light yet flavorful bowl of comfort.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1 lb (450g) carrots, peeled and sliced
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken stock
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large saucepan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and garlic; cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Stir in ground coriander and cook for 1 minute to release the aroma.
  3. Add the sliced carrots and stock. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20â€“25 minutes, or until carrots are tender.
  4. Remove from heat and blend the soup with an immersion blender or in batches until smooth.
  5. Return the soup to the pan, season with salt and pepper to taste, and heat through.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with chopped fresh coriander.

Tips

  • For a creamier version, add a splash of coconut milk or cream after blending.
  • Use fresh coriander stalks while cooking for extra flavor, then garnish with the leaves.
  • This soup can be stored in the fridge for up to 3 days or frozen for later use.
