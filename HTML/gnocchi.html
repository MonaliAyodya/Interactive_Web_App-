Gnocchi

Description

Gnocchi are soft Italian dumplings made from potatoes, flour, and egg. They're light, pillowy, and perfect with a variety of sauces like marinara, pesto, or brown butter sage.

Ingredients

  • 500g potatoes (russet or starchy)
  • 150g all-purpose flour (plus extra for dusting)
  • 1 egg, lightly beaten
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

  1. Boil the potatoes with skin on until tender. Peel while still warm and mash until smooth.
  2. Mix the mashed potatoes with flour, egg, and a pinch of salt to form a dough.
  3. Divide the dough into portions and roll into long ropes. Cut into 1-inch pieces and optionally press with a fork to shape.
  4. Bring salted water to a boil. Cook gnocchi in batches until they float to the surface (2â€“3 minutes).
  5. Serve with your favorite sauce, such as marinara, butter sage, or creamy cheese sauce.

Tips

  • Don't overwork the dough or the gnocchi will be tough.
  • Chill the dough if it's too soft to roll.
