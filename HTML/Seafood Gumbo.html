Seafood Gumbo

Description

This flavorful Seafood Gumbo is a hearty, rich, and spicy dish that brings together shrimp, crab, and other seafood in a thick, savory roux-based broth. It’s perfect for any seafood lover looking for a comforting, Louisiana-style meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb shrimp, peeled and deveined
  • 1 lb crabmeat
  • 1/2 lb smoked sausage, sliced
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 bell pepper, chopped
  • 2 stalks celery, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (14.5 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 4 cups seafood or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1/2 cup green onions, chopped
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • Cooked rice, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the flour and cook, stirring constantly, for 10–15 minutes, until the roux is a dark brown color.
  2. Add the onions, bell pepper, and celery to the pot. Cook for 5–7 minutes, until softened.
  3. Stir in the garlic and cook for 1 more minute.
  4. Add the diced tomatoes, seafood or chicken broth, thyme, paprika, cayenne pepper, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes.
  5. Add the shrimp, crabmeat, and smoked sausage to the pot. Simmer for an additional 10 minutes, until the seafood is cooked through.
  6. Remove the bay leaves and discard. Stir in the green onions and parsley.
  7. Serve the gumbo over cooked rice. Garnish with extra parsley and green onions if desired.

Tips

  • For a thicker gumbo, you can add more roux or let it simmer longer to reduce the liquid.
  • If you prefer a spicier dish, increase the cayenne pepper or add a dash of hot sauce.
  • Gumbo can be made ahead and stored in the fridge for up to 3 days, and it actually gets better with time!
