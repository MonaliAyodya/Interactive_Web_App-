Lemon Meringue Pie

Description

This classic Lemon Meringue Pie features a crisp pie crust, tangy lemon filling, and a fluffy meringue topping. It's a delightful dessert that combines sweetness and tartness in every bite!

Ingredients

  • 1 pie crust (store-bought or homemade)
  • 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup cornstarch
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 1/2 cups water
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 1/2 cup fresh lemon juice (about 2–3 lemons)
  • 1 tablespoon lemon zest
  • 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
  • 1/4 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large egg whites
  • 1/2 teaspoon cream of tartar
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar (for meringue)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Bake the pie crust according to the package directions, or until golden brown if homemade. Let it cool while you prepare the filling.
  2. In a medium saucepan, whisk together 1 1/2 cups sugar, cornstarch, and salt. Gradually add the water and cook over medium heat, whisking constantly until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil (about 5–7 minutes).
  3. In a separate bowl, whisk together the egg yolks. Slowly pour a small amount of the hot sugar mixture into the yolks to temper them, whisking constantly. Then, gradually pour the yolk mixture back into the saucepan, whisking constantly. Cook for an additional 2–3 minutes until thickened.
  4. Remove the saucepan from the heat and stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, butter, and vanilla extract. Stir until the butter is melted and the mixture is smooth. Pour the lemon filling into the cooled pie crust.
  5. In a clean bowl, beat the egg whites with the cream of tartar until soft peaks form. Gradually add 1/4 cup sugar and continue beating until stiff peaks form.
  6. Spread the meringue over the lemon filling, making sure it touches the edges of the pie crust to seal it. Use a spatula to create decorative peaks in the meringue.
  7. Bake the pie in the preheated oven for 10–12 minutes, or until the meringue is golden brown. Remove from the oven and let it cool completely before serving.

Tips

  • If the meringue starts to shrink away from the edges during baking, it's a sign that it wasn't sealed properly to the crust.
  • Let the pie cool for several hours before serving for the best texture and flavor.
  • If you're using a store-bought pie crust, ensure it's fully baked before filling it with the lemon mixture to avoid sogginess.
