Vietnamese Noodle Soup (Pho)

Description

This Vietnamese Pho is a fragrant, flavorful noodle soup made with rich broth, tender beef or chicken, and rice noodles. Itâ€™s a popular comfort food that's perfect for any time of the year.

Ingredients

  • 1 lb beef bones or chicken bones (for broth)
  • 1 onion, peeled and halved
  • 1 ginger root, peeled and sliced
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 3-4 cloves
  • 1 tablespoon coriander seeds
  • 2 star anise
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 8 cups water
  • 1/2 lb rice noodles (pho noodles)
  • 1/2 lb beef sirloin or chicken breast (sliced thinly)
  • Fresh herbs: cilantro, Thai basil, and mint leaves
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Bean sprouts
  • Chili peppers, sliced (optional)
  • Sriracha or hoisin sauce (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, combine the bones (beef or chicken) with water. Bring it to a boil over high heat, then reduce to a simmer. Skim the impurities that float to the surface.
  2. Char the onion and ginger under a broiler or on a grill until slightly blackened. Add them to the pot along with the cinnamon sticks, cloves, coriander seeds, and star anise.
  3. Simmer the broth for at least 3 hours, adding more water if needed to keep it covered. The longer it simmers, the richer the broth will be.
  4. After simmering, strain the broth through a fine-mesh sieve to remove the solids. Season with fish sauce and sugar to taste. Keep the broth hot.
  5. Cook the rice noodles according to the package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  6. To serve, place a portion of the cooked noodles in a bowl. Top with thinly sliced beef or chicken, then pour the hot broth over the top, which will cook the meat.
  7. Garnish with fresh herbs, bean sprouts, chili peppers, lime wedges, and optional sauces like sriracha or hoisin.
  8. Serve immediately and enjoy!

Tips

  • For a richer broth, you can roast the bones in the oven before adding them to the pot.
  • If you prefer a spicier pho, add more chili peppers or use a spicy sriracha sauce.
  • Feel free to customize your pho with additional toppings like fried onions or bok choy.
